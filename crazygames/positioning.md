# COIL vs. Sticky Orbit — positioning & how we win

## The competitor

**Sticky Orbit** (crazygames.com/game/sticky-orbit) — a physics arcade game: leap
between the gravity fields of rotating planets, time jumps to keep momentum and
build combos; a Survival Mode where you outrun a massive growing sun that consumes
everything.

**Signals (as of May 2026):** ~720 ratings, ~643 likes, **8.9/10**, **released May
2026** (brand new).

### What that data tells us
- **Not entrenched.** It launched this month with modest reach (~720 rates). There
  is no dominant incumbent owning this niche — the race is open.
- **Demand is validated.** Someone just proved players enjoy the one-tap orbital-
  timing + outrun-the-hazard loop on CrazyGames. That de-risks COIL's concept.
- **Quality bar is real but beatable.** 8.9/10 says "execute well or don't bother,"
  not "impossible to displace."

**Conclusion:** This is close to the best-case scenario — a freshly-proven niche
with no king yet. The job is to out-execute and out-position, not to invent.

## Where we overlap (be honest)
One-tap orbital timing → combo chaining → outrun a rising lethal hazard (their sun ≈
our void/lava). The core arcade fantasy is the same. We must not pretend otherwise;
we must make sure COIL *reads and plays* as its own, deeper thing.

## Where COIL is different — and better

| Axis | Sticky Orbit (apparent) | COIL — our edge |
|---|---|---|
| Fairness | Momentum physics; can feel arbitrary | **Provably honest gate** (CI test: 0 dishonest gates) + lead-predicted moving nodes → "I lost because *I* erred" |
| Meta-progression | Survival/score | **Collection** (characters/trails/worlds, rarity + *earned* skill-unlocks), XP/levels, **Star Vault**, achievements |
| Return hooks | — | Seeded **Daily Challenge**, daily missions, login streaks |
| Modes | Survival | **Endless + Daily + Zen** (no-fail, calm) |
| Identity | Abstract planets/sun | A **cosmic creature with a face** you collect and bond with |
| Feel/polish | — | Adaptive music, animated lava, parallax depth, reduced-motion, FX scaling |

These are exactly the **D1/D7 retention levers** the CrazyGames algorithm promotes
on. Sticky Orbit looks like a lean survival toy; COIL is the same satisfying loop
wrapped in a retention machine and more charm.

## The battleground: thumbnail CTR + first 20 seconds
Concept similarity is a *marketing* problem, not a gameplay one. Two rules:

1. **Don't look like a clone in the grid.** Sticky Orbit's visual hook is
   *planets + a sun*. COIL's must be *one cute creature + a single glowing gate +
   a vertical neon climb*. Different silhouette, different palette energy. See
   `asset-prompts.md`.
2. **Don't compete on their keywords.** Lead with *"cosmic creature collection
   arcade"* and *"perfect tap,"* not *"orbit"* / *"outrun the sun."* Our title
   (COIL) is already distinct from "Sticky Orbit."

## Win conditions (do these)
1. **Benchmark by playing Sticky Orbit.** Note its difficulty ramp, juice, and what
   it lacks (almost certainly: deep meta-progression, a daily route, a no-fail mode,
   character identity). Beat it on those.
2. **Differentiated thumbnail** (3 variants to A/B — `asset-prompts.md`).
3. **Make our signature hooks loud in session 1:** the perfect-gate feedback, the
   Star Vault tease, and an explicit "Reach 500 m to unlock Void" carrot —
   progression a survival clone doesn't have.

## Signature differentiator (SHIPPED)
**Constellation chains** — three marked gates appear in sequence; perfect all three
in a row to complete an in-run mini-objective. Completing one pays coins + a
celebration and counts toward lifetime progress that unlocks the **Star Lace**
world (10 constellations) and two achievements (STARWEAVER / CONSTELLER). It
compounds our two advantages — provable fairness + collection depth — into a
single hook a pure survival game can't trivially copy. Built ON the honest-gate
system, so the gate-honesty test still passes (0 dishonest gates). In-world it
shows star-marked nodes linked by a dotted constellation line + a HUD tracker.
